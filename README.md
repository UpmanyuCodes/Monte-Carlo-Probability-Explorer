# Monte Carlo Probability Explorer

Monte Carlo Probability Explorer is a collection of probability simulations designed to investigate fundamental concepts in probability and statistics through computational experiments. Using repeated random sampling, these simulations provide intuitive insights into convergence, estimation, and counterintuitive probabilistic phenomena.

## Simulations Included : 

This repository contains a series of Monte Carlo experiments exploring a variety of probability concepts:

* **Coin Toss Simulation** – Demonstrates how the experimental probability of obtaining heads converges toward the theoretical probability of **0.5**, illustrating the **Law of Large Numbers**.
* **Dice Roll Simulation** – Shows how the relative frequencies of each face approach their expected probability of **1/6** as the number of rolls increases.
* **Monte Carlo Estimation of π** – Uses random sampling to estimate the value of **π**, highlighting how repeated trials improve the accuracy of statistical estimates.
* **Birthday Paradox Simulation** – Explores the surprisingly high probability that two individuals in a group share the same birthday.
* **Monty Hall Problem Simulation** – Investigates the famous game show paradox, demonstrating through simulation that switching doors yields a winning probability of approximately **2/3**, despite the common misconception that the odds become **50-50**.

## Objective

The objective of this project is to develop an intuitive understanding of probability through simulation. By translating theoretical concepts into computational experiments, these simulations allow abstract ideas to be observed, tested, and better understood.

## Key Takeaway

> Probability is often unintuitive, but repeated experimentation through Monte Carlo methods reveals the patterns and principles governing random processes.

Through these simulations, abstract statistical concepts become observable experiments, making probability theory more accessible, interactive, and engaging.
