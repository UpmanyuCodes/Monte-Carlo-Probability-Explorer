# Monte Carlo Explorer: The Law of Large Numbers

Monte Carlo Explorer is a collection of probability simulations designed to demonstrate the **Law of Large Numbers** through computational experiments. By repeatedly performing random trials, these simulations illustrate how experimental outcomes gradually converge toward their theoretical expectations as the number of observations increases.

This repository includes a variety of Monte Carlo experiments, ranging from simple probability models to more intriguing statistical phenomena:

* **Coin Toss Simulation** – Visualizes the convergence of the experimental probability of heads toward the theoretical probability of 0.5.
* **Dice Roll Simulation** – Demonstrates how the relative frequencies of each face approach their expected probability of 1/6.
* **Monte Carlo Estimation of π** – Uses random sampling to estimate the value of π and observe the accuracy improvement with increasing trials.
* **Birthday Paradox Simulation** – Explores the surprising probability that two individuals in a group share the same birthday.

## Objective

The primary objective of this project is to provide an intuitive understanding of the **Law of Large Numbers** using simulations and visualizations. While individual experiments may exhibit significant randomness, increasing the number of trials reveals stable long-term patterns that align closely with theoretical predictions.

## Technologies Used

* Python
* Matplotlib
* Random module

## Key Takeaway

> As the number of independent trials increases, experimental results tend to converge toward their expected theoretical values.

Through these simulations, Monte Carlo methods transform abstract statistical concepts into observable and interactive experiments, making probability theory easier to understand and appreciate.
